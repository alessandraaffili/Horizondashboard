# Horizondashboard
Horizon AI Literacy Dashboard — what it is and how to use it

What this is

This dashboard is a snapshot of where our people are across the five Horizon AI profiles, based on the Profile Finder questionnaire. It's designed to help us identify where the biggest opportunities for intervention are, track progress against our OKRs, and make decisions about where to focus effort.

It's built from 688 responses collected up to April 22 2026. We'll refresh it every 1–2 months as new data comes in.


What it's not

It's not a live system — it won't update automatically. It's a point-in-time picture, and we should treat it as one. It also doesn't tell us why people are at a given level, or what's stopped them progressing. That's our job to interpret and act on.

It's also not a complete picture of the whole organisation. We're measuring against people who have taken the survey, not total headcount. Participation varies significantly by department — India Operations in particular has very low uptake, which means we should be cautious about drawing conclusions there.


The five tabs — what each one is for

OKR Progress — start here. This shows where we stand against our three key results for 2026. The bars show current position, baseline, and target. Use this to orient any conversation about priorities.

Profile Distribution — shows how the organisation is spread across Observer, Seeker, Navigator, Adventurer and Pathfinder, both overall and by department. Use this to identify which departments have the most people clustered at lower levels.

Barrier Analysis — this is the most actionable tab for intervention design. It shows which specific behaviours people are already demonstrating and which they're not, at the two most critical transitions: Seeker→Navigator and Navigator→Adventurer. Use this to design targeted interventions rather than generic ones.

Departments — a full breakdown of participation rates and profile outcomes by department. Use this to spot where low participation might be masking a bigger problem, and where the highest-potential cohorts are.

People Lens — shows the manager vs non-manager split and what happens when people retake the survey. Use this when thinking about who your intervention targets should be and whether your approach is reaching the right people.


Three things worth knowing before you dig in

The Navigator cohort is your priority pipeline. There are 130 people sitting one level below Adventurer. The barrier analysis shows clearly what's holding them back — they understand AI value but aren't yet sharing it outward. That's your design brief.

Peer learning is the single biggest gap at Seeker level. 69% of Seekers are not participating in peer learning. This isn't a skills problem — it's a structural one. Interventions that create peer learning moments will have the broadest reach.

Managers are behind non-managers. This is counterintuitive and worth paying attention to. Any intervention that relies on managers modelling behaviour needs to account for this.


How to use it in practice

Open the file in your browser (download it first if needed — it won't render directly in Google Drive). Navigate between tabs using the menu at the top. The data is fixed — you can't filter or drill down further within the dashboard itself. If you want to explore a specific question the dashboard doesn't answer, bring it to the team and we can run a deeper analysis.

The dashboard will be updated when we run the next data refresh. Any significant changes in the numbers will be flagged at that point.
